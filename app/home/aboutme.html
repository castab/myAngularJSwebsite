About Me

I'm currently attending California State University, Fresno in pursuit of my first undergraduate degree in Electrical Engineering. I feel that my strengths are in communication systems and signal processing. I also have passion in Internet of Things, power efficiency, and networked computing.

My other hobbies include music, stage lighting and sound, diy electronics, shortwave radio, and drones.

Why Engineering?

I've worked retail for most of my working life and have come to find it unfulfilling. Yeah, it paid the bills, but offered no real goal for me. I'm not talking about rising through management - I'm talking about growing intellectually and consciously. I enjoyed the company of my co-workers but after some time, the work was just repetitive and always came to the bottom line. Don't get me wrong, there should always be a bottom line, but I knew that I had it in me to become something more. I had my hobbies and have tried majoring in IT before, but now it was time to make good on my passions and get an Engineering degree.

Only in these recent years, I've come to realize how blind I was to how the physical world works and am now enlightened; I can see things I've never realized and can manipulate them for the better. One of my biggest leaps into enlightenment was the Fourier Transform and the power it possesses and seemingly endless applications. I could go on and on about the Fourier Transform as well as its close mathematical functions: the Laplace Transform and Z-Transform... but maybe for another time.

So here I am, about to finish my first undergraduate degree in Electrical Engineering!
