Addressable High-Power RGB LEDs
Eureka Moment!

Interestingly enough, the vision for this project came to me during a gathering with some of my colleagues. I remember watching the various decorations and colors... It occurred to me that I could use a MOSFET to control the current flow of a higher voltage source using a low voltage PWM signal. The idea was just too good to let go from my mind so I scribbled notes on one of the napkins!

Thankfully enough, a group of us got together and discussed the idea as I was scribbling out the rough draft of what would become a working circuit.
WS2811 Addressable LEDs

The concept of addressable LEDs was still a brand new idea that became cheap enough to reach consumers. Shortly after my involvement with the Wild West Shootout Game, I just couldn't get enough of these things and thought to myself, what if the same idea was applied to 10-, 30-, 50-, or even 100-watt LEDs? So, using a little circuit know-how, I prototyped a controllable 50-watt RGB LED.
