The Beginnings

My very first experience with any kind of web development came from tinkering around with the ESP8266 WiFi-enabled microcontroller. Before I knew what APIs were (much less, write an API), my default interface with the microcontroller was a very simple website that included a simple dialog and a drop-down box that would turn one of its IO pins HIGH or LOW; triggering an attached relay.

Fast-forwarding to a crazy summer, I stumbled upon Lynda.com and found a beginner's course on PHP! By the end of the summer, I was enthralled with what could be done with a little bit of server-side processing and some persistant storage such as SQL.

Over time, I picked up a good chunk of HTML and CSS, but I would have to say that my biggest leap was my internship with Target. Throughout my internship, I picked up AngularJS and I couldn't believe how far behind I was with respect to data/session binding. To drive this further, AngularJS isn't even one of the most recent frameworks!

I made a small demo showcasing some of the newer skills I gained over the past year or so.

Using a mix of AngularJS, PHP, and MySQL, I've created a dynamic page from which data is fetched from an SQL server and wrapped into a JSON payload by PHP and then shot here for display.
